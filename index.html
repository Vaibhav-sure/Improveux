Issues vs. fixes

Five findings from the current site, paired with a direct fix for each.

Identified issues

Scrolling marquee banner

Auto-scrolling promo text runs continuously at the top, competing with the hero for attention.

"Free" claim vs. hidden fee

Hero says "kickstart your career for FREE," but the ₹99 certificate fee only appears several sections down.

Trust signals repeated, not proven

The MSME registration number appears 6+ times, but there's no named testimonial, review, or student photo behind "415+ enrolled."

Nav mixes site sections with external links

Top nav blends in-page anchors (About, Features) with an external link (Emogi) and account actions (Login, Apply) at equal weight.

No pricing or FAQ on the homepage

Cost and process questions are only answered on individual domain pages, not on the page most visitors land on first.

Proposed fixes

Make the ticker pausable, or remove it

Replace with a static trust strip, or add pause-on-hover and respect prefers-reduced-motion.

State the fee next to "free"

"Free internship. Certificate issuance is ₹99." One honest line beats discovering it five scrolls later.

Swap repetition for proof

Cut MSME mentions to 1–2 placements; add named testimonials, LinkedIn certificate posts, or a reviews widget.

Separate navigation from account actions

Keep About/Features/Collaborations as page nav; move Emogi to the footer; set Login/Apply apart as the primary CTA pair.

Add a homepage pricing/FAQ block

Pull the strong FAQ content already on domain pages up to the homepage so first-time visitors don't have to dig.

Where to start

Ordered by effort-to-impact — the first two need no design work, only copy changes.

  • 1

    Disclose the fee in the hero

    Zero design cost. Removes the single biggest trust risk on the page.

  • 2

    Replace repeated badges with real proof

    Swap one MSME mention for a named testimonial or certificate screenshot.

  • 3

    Restructure the top nav

    Group by function: page links, then account actions, then external link in the footer.

  • 4

    Bring FAQ content to the homepage

    Reuse existing copy from domain pages — no new writing required.

Based on a content and structure review of virtualworks.space (homepage and Web Development domain page), July 2026. No live user testing was conducted — findings are heuristic, not behavioral data.
